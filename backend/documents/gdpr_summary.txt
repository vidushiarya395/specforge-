GDPR - GENERAL DATA PROTECTION REGULATION SUMMARY

WHAT IS GDPR:
EU law that protects personal data and privacy of individuals.
Applies to any product that handles data of EU citizens.

KEY PRINCIPLES:
1. LAWFULNESS - Must have legal basis to process data
2. PURPOSE LIMITATION - Collect data only for specific purposes
3. DATA MINIMISATION - Collect only what is necessary
4. ACCURACY - Keep data accurate and up to date
5. STORAGE LIMITATION - Don't keep data longer than needed
6. INTEGRITY & CONFIDENTIALITY - Protect data with security measures

USER RIGHTS UNDER GDPR:
- Right to access their data
- Right to delete their data (right to be forgotten)
- Right to correct inaccurate data
- Right to data portability
- Right to withdraw consent

CONSENT REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be freely given, specific, informed
- Must be as easy to withdraw as to give
- Pre-ticked boxes are NOT valid consent
- Separate consent for each purpose

DATA BREACH REQUIREMENTS:
- Must notify authorities within 72 hours
- Must notify affected users without undue delay
- Must keep records of all breaches

PENALTIES:
- Up to 4% of global annual revenue
- Or 20 million euros, whichever is higher

COMMON GDPR VIOLATIONS IN APPS:
- No privacy policy
- Tracking users without consent
- Storing passwords in plain text
- Sharing user data with third parties without consent
- Not allowing users to delete their accounts
